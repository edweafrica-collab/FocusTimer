Version 2.0.0 (The Redesign)

22 January 2026

A massive overhaul introducing a complete visual redesign, advanced event scheduling, and smart multi-screen features.

Headline Features:

  • Event Scheduler — Plan your entire event with a drag-and-drop playlist. Insert, edit, and delete slots on the fly.
  • Smart Overtime — Timer counts up with a negative sign (e.g., -02:30) and the screen pulses red to signal urgency.
  • Dynamic Multi-Screen — cueTime now automatically detects second screens. Plug in a projector mid-event, and we'll ask to launch the Viewer on it.
  • Pro Controls — Restart completed events, safe-delete active slots, and manual play/pause overrides.
  • Visual Refresh — A polished dark mode UI with glassmorphism effects and smooth animations.

Fixes & Improvements:

  • Larger default window size with custom scrollbars.
  • Modal confirmations for critical actions (Start Next, Delete).
  • Improved robustness against accidental closures.

Note from the Devs:

We finally added the red pulse you asked for. Please don't give your speakers a heart attack.

Version 1.0.0

4 January 2026

This is the initial public release of cueTime!

The Origin Story:

cueTime was born out of frustration. We've all been there—you're presenting at a live event, and someone starts waving frantically from the back of the room. "Wrap it up!" they mime, while you try to maintain composure. We thought: there has to be a better way. A calmer way. A way that doesn't involve hand signals or panic.

Features:

  • Dual-window system — Dashboard for control, Viewer for display
  • Quick time presets — 5, 10, 15, 20, 30, 45, 60, 90 minutes
  • Custom time input — Set any duration you need
  • Warning states — Yellow at 5 minutes, red when time's up
  • Clean typography — Large, readable countdown display
  • Always on top — Viewer stays visible above other windows
  • Dark mode ready — Easy on the eyes in dim venues

Built With:

  • Electron for cross-platform desktop support
  • Pure JavaScript, no heavy frameworks
  • Love for presenters everywhere

Parting Shot:

If you like cueTime, share it with your AV team. They'll thank you. Or at least stop waving signs at you.
